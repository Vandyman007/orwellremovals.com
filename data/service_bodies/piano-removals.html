The Right Way to Move a Piano

Pianos are awkward, top-heavy and surprisingly fragile, and a single slip can damage both the instrument and your home, not to mention someone’s back. Our trained crew treats every piano removals job as a planned operation rather than a lift-and-hope: we use proper piano trolleys, thick padding and ratchet straps, and we walk the route through your home first so doorways, tight corners and stairs are managed calmly and safely.

Preparation is what separates a smooth move from a costly one. We measure narrow hallways, check stair widths and landing turns, and remove obstacles before the instrument leaves its spot. Floors and skirting are protected as we go, and the piano is kept upright and balanced throughout. If you are also relocating the rest of the household, our home removals team can fold the piano into the wider plan.

Uprights, grands and digitals

From a compact upright in a terraced front room to a baby grand that has to come down a flight of stairs, we assess each instrument and match the right approach and equipment to it. Uprights are strapped and trolleyed to stay stable; grands and baby grands often have their legs and lyre carefully removed and the body laid on a padded skid board so it travels secure and well supported.

Digital pianos and electric keyboards are lighter but still deserve care, as knocks can crack casings or unsettle delicate electronics. We protect the casework, secure the lid and keys, and move steadily with no rushing and no shortcuts. Heavier instruments are handled by enough crew to share the load properly, and we always confirm access at both ends before lifting so there are no unwelcome surprises mid-move.

Part of a bigger move, or on its own

We move pianos as part of a full house relocation or as a standalone job when the piano is the only thing travelling. Plenty of customers call us simply to shift an inherited upright between rooms, take a grand to a new home across town, or load one for a recital, and we are happy to handle these single-item jobs with the same trolleys, padding and route planning we bring to a complete home removal.

If the instrument needs to go into store for a while, perhaps during a renovation or a gap between properties, our clean, dry storage keeps it safe, since pianos far prefer a stable indoor environment to a damp garage. We can also wrap and protect surrounding furniture as part of our professional packing service, so nothing nearby gets marked while the piano is on the move.
